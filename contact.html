Common Questions

Before Your First Visit

What should I bring to my first visit?

A valid photo ID, your insurance card, and a list of any current medications. If you have recent lab results or records from a prior physician, bring those too — or have them faxed to (212) 555-0181 in advance.

Do you accept walk-ins?

We reserve a limited number of same-day slots for urgent concerns, but we strongly recommend calling ahead so we can confirm availability and have your intake ready. Walk-ins are welcome but may face wait times.

How does telehealth work?

After booking, you'll receive a secure link via email or text. Click it at your appointment time — no app or download required. Works on any smartphone, tablet, or computer with a camera.

What if I don't have insurance?

We offer transparent self-pay pricing and can discuss sliding-scale options based on individual circumstances. We believe cost should never prevent someone from accessing primary care. Call us to talk through options.

How far in advance should I book?

New patient comprehensive visits are typically available within 1–3 business days. For same-day sick visits, call us in the morning — we hold slots daily. Annual physicals can be booked up to six months out.

Can I request a specific doctor?

Absolutely. Use the "Preferred Provider" field in the booking form to request a specific physician. We'll do our best to match you, and once matched, you'll see the same doctor at every visit.
