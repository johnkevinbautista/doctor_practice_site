Meet Our Doctors

Internal Medicine · Primary Care

Dr. Sarah Chen, MD

MD, Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons · Board Certified, ABIM · 12 years in practice
Internal Medicine
New York Presbyterian / Columbia
Accepting new patients

Dr. Chen completed her medical degree at Columbia University and her residency at New York-Presbyterian Hospital, where she developed a deep interest in the relationship between lifestyle, prevention, and long-term outcomes. She joined Meridian Health in 2016 because she wanted to practice medicine on her own terms — thoughtfully, without rushing.

Her patients describe her as thorough, warm, and exceptionally good at explaining complex medical information in language that actually makes sense. She has a particular expertise in preventive cardiology, diabetes management, and helping patients navigate the gap between knowing what's healthy and actually making it happen.

"Outside the office, I'm a passionate home cook and an amateur marathoner. I believe that joy and health are deeply connected — and I try to bring that perspective into every visit."

Preventive Cardiology Diabetes Management Hypertension Lifestyle Medicine Women's Preventive Health

Family Medicine

Dr. Marcus Webb, MD

MD, Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine · Board Certified, ABFM · 15 years in practice
Family Medicine
Johns Hopkins Hospital Residency
Accepting new patients

Dr. Webb trained at one of the world's most respected academic medical institutions and brings that intellectual rigor to every patient encounter. After residency, he practiced in both academic and community settings before finding his home at Meridian — a place where, as he puts it, "I can finally spend the time that good medicine actually requires."

Known for his directness, his ability to synthesize complex information, and a genuine gift for putting nervous patients at ease, Dr. Webb sees patients across all age groups and has a particular focus on men's health, cardiovascular wellness, and preventive medicine. He's the kind of doctor who sends follow-up messages after difficult visits — because he's still thinking about you.

"I grew up watching family members navigate a healthcare system that often felt indifferent. That experience drives everything I do. My patients deserve better than indifferent — and I intend to deliver it."

Men's Health Cardiovascular Wellness Family Medicine Preventive Screening Cholesterol Management

Preventive Medicine · Women's Health

Dr. Priya Nair, MD

MD, Stanford University School of Medicine · Board Certified, ABPM · 9 years in practice
Preventive Medicine
Stanford / UCSF Fellowship
Accepting new patients

Dr. Nair trained in preventive medicine at Stanford and completed a fellowship in women's health at UCSF, where she developed specialized expertise in hormonal health, reproductive medicine, and the science of longevity. She's motivated by a simple conviction: most chronic disease is preventable, and the earlier patients and physicians work together, the better the outcomes.

Her patients describe her as empowering. She takes the time to explain not just what to do, but why — drawing on the latest evidence while keeping the conversation grounded in what's realistic for each person's actual life. She's passionate about closing the gender gap in medicine and ensuring women receive the nuanced, thorough care they've historically been denied.

"I believe that feeling well — truly well, not just not-sick — is something every patient should experience. That's what I work toward with every single person I see."

The Meridian Standard

Regardless of which physician you see, you'll always experience the same core commitments.

40–60 Minute Appointments

Every visit is long enough to cover everything on your mind. No clock-watching, no cutting corners.

Pre-Read Patient Charts

Your doctor reviews your history before the appointment starts — so you spend time on care, not catch-up.

Direct Secure Messaging

Message your physician directly through the patient portal. Thoughtful responses, not automated deflection.

Continuity of Care

Your assigned physician is your physician — for years, not just today's visit. That continuity is the foundation of trust.
